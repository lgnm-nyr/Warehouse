This is the how-to use page! You can browse through the guides we have made for each tool here or search for a specific one!

There are also included warnings and precautions for each tool as well!

Angle-Grinder

Firstly ensure the workpiece is secured safely. Adopt a stable balanced stance when handing the tool that allows full control of the machine. When cutting, always cut in a straight line, making sure not to trap or pinch the wheel into the cut. Do not try to force the wheel through the workpiece either and also do not apply pressure from the side of the wheel. A grinding disc should not be used to explicitly cut off areas from a work piece. Likewise, do not grind on an area of cut-off as it will not be strong enough. After completing usage of the tool, wait for the wheel to finish turning and then set down the tool. Never apply pressure in an act to slow the wheel down faster.

  • Must Wear Eye Protection
  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
  • Hadn Protectors such as Rigger Gloves to be Worn with this Item
  • Safety Footwear is Required for this Tool

Belt Sander

Before handling the belt sander, ensure that clothing is kept well clear from the sander. Then the sander can be turned on, allowing the motor to reach full speed. Using two hands, one on the trigger and the other on the forward handle, ease the sander onto the workpiece. The rear part of the roller should make contact first and then followed by the rest of the sander. Maintain a firm grip on the sander as the wood will grab at the sander and pull it away. For optimal use, work the sander with the grain of the wood, avoiding keeping it in one place. To turn off the sander, come off the trigger and wait for the belt to stop rotating. Then unplug the tool and wait for it to cool down before storing it.

  • Must Wear Eye Protection
  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
  • Hand Protectors such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item
  • There is a Medium Level Vibration Risk

Chainsaw

First scan your work area to ensure there is space to operate the saw and that there are no obstructions. Then check that the chain has good tension to it. To test this, see if the chain can be tugged from the guide bar without disengagement from the bar. After this, prime the saw by turning the power on but not revving it up. Once in a steady position, press the trigger to start moving the chain. Always ensure that you cut off to the side or angled away from you to minimize injury if kickback occurs. When making a cut, keep the throttle on throughout the time of the cut. To turn the saw off, go off the throttle, unplug and let the saw cool down. It can then be stored safely.

  • Must Wear Eye Protection
  • Must Have Protectors Against High Noise Levels
  • Hand Protectors such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item
  • This is a High-Risk Tool

Electric Drill

The drill must be charged before use. To remove the battery, press the unlock buttons on either side of the battery and slide out the pack with care. To charge the battery, plug the charger into the power source through connection. Charging will take around 1 - 2 hours. A flashing green indicator lamp shows charging in progress. Charging completion is shown by the green lamp staying lit. To install the battery back to the drill, push the battery back into the drill handle until a ‘clicking’ sound will indicate it is secured. The trigger switch on the drill is progressive and allows the user to start drilling slowly. The torque of the drill can be pre selected by turning the control sleeve at the front of the drill from 1 being lowest torque to 15 for larger screws or tougher materials.

  • Must Wear Eye Protection
  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
  • Hand Protectors such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item
  • This is a High-Risk Tool

Jigsaw

To fit the blade to the saw, turn the tool upside down and loosen the screw that secures the blade. Then insert the blade into the blade chunk, pushing the blade firmly and finally tightening the screw. Make sure the teeth are facing forwards from the tool. The blade can be in a horizontal or vertical position and this can be adjusted with the switch on the side. A ‘clicking’ sound indicates the blade has been fixed into the desired position correctly. When the blade is secured, ensure the blade guard is refitted. Before switching the tool on, keep the front of the sole plate on the work. Then turn the saw on by plugging it in and switching it on. Stagger the speed as the tool is brought in contact with the work piece. When operating the saw use a vice or a clamp to maintain steadiness when working. It is also advised that a stable stance is adopted that provides a good view of the off cut while maintaining distance from the blade.

  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
  • Hand Protectors such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item

Mitre Saw

Firstly set the saw on a stable workbench and then if needed fit a dust extraction unit. If one is not used, make sure the work area is ventilated. To fit the saw to the workbench, pull out the 2 rear base stabilisers until they are in contact with the bench and then tighten with the retaining screw. For further stability screws can be inserted through the 4 holes in each corner of the saw to directly secure it to the bench. When cutting longer pieces of timber, prepare a side support that is the exact same height as the bed of the unit. When making adjustments to either the bevel angles or mitre, after sliding the rear fences, they must be reset to ensure they support the workpiece and do not intrude onto the blade. Once setup is complete, the saw can be started by squeezing the switch trigger. To stop release the trigger.

  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
  • Hand Protection such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item

Nail Gun

To use strip nails with the gun, slide the follower knob to the cartridge body base until locked into position. Then insert the strip of nails into the bottom of the hollow cartridge with the nail points facing forward. Afterwards, press the button in the centre of the follower knob and it will hold the strip in the correct place. Likewise, to fit brad nails, follow the same procedure but insert upto a maximum of 2 strips of brad nails. After inserting the desired nails, insert a fully charged battery at the base of the tool’s handle and ensure it is secured. When in operation, take care in having the safety tip in contact with the fixing surface and that the tip is fully depressed. Afterwards, squeeze the trigger to fire the nail into the workpiece.

Orbital Sander

Firstly make sure all work is free from nails, screws or other protrusions. If these cannot be removed, secure them below the surface level of the surface. Make sure the machine is switched off and not connected to a power supply and then turn the sander upside down. Fit the sander with the desired abrasive. Typically, start with a medium grade and then would through to a finer finish. The abrasive disc is held on the backing pad with a hook and loop system. To place the abrasive on, align the extraction holes on the back pad with the ones on the abrasive, then simply push them together. There is an inbuilt dust extraction system within the sander and to remove the dust bag, simply slide the bag back. To refit, follow the reverse. Speed controls on the control dial vary from 1 (4,500rpm) - 6 (12,000rpm). When the sander is operational, but there is no connection to the surface the rotational speed will be low. This is due to the inbuilt safety brake.

Rotary Hammer Drill

To install the bits of a rotary drill, hold the chuck at the front of the tool and then push it towards the rear. While keeping the chuck inplace, pick the bit with the free hand and insert into the chuck. Wrist the bit until it sits into the seat of the chuck. With the bit attachment installed, connect the rotary hammer to power with the battery and ensure the correct part is being used for the job. Then align the tip of the drill with the target area and secure placement. It is good practice to create a pilot hole first and then switch from drilling to hammering and drilling to go through the rest of the workpiece.

  • Hand Protectors such as Rigger Gloves are to be Worn with this Item

Router

Make sure the correct cutter is equipped for the right job for the router. Firstly switch off the machine and unplug it before fitting the cutter. Grip the spindle with the spanner provided and slide the spindle lock plate to stop it turning. Then use the second spanner to open the collet which will allow the cutter’s shank to be placed into the jaws for tightening into place. To set the depth of cut, twist the max nut on the threaded pillar till it is as near to the base plate as possible. Then release the locking lever and lower the tool until the workpiece is in contact with the cutter. After placing the cutter, lock the body in place and then turn the depth adjustment knob until the slide depth resets on one of the top three depth stop screws. Finally raise the depth stop to the required amount for cutting with the scale, making sure the nut on the threaded pillar touches the lug.

  • Must Have Protection Against High Noise Levels
